KIMCHIJEON

Kimchiejeon, or kimchi pancakes, is a beloved Korean dish that combines fermented kimchi with a savoury batter of flour, water, and optional seasonings. This delightful treat is pan-fried until golden and crispy on the outside while remaining soft and flavorful. The bold, tangy taste of kimchi and the pancake's crisp texture make it a popular snack or appetizer. Often enjoyed as part of a meal or with makgeolli (Korean rice wine), kimchiejeon is both satisfying and simple to prepare. Variations can include additional ingredients like green onions, seafood, or thinly sliced vegetables, adding depth to its flavour. Kimchiejeon embodies the essence of Korean cuisine—comforting, flavorful, and versatile. It is not just a dish but a testament to the culinary creativity inspired by kimchi, a staple in Korean culture and tradition. Perfect for any occasion!

Prep Time:

10 mins

Cook Time:

15 mins

Servings:

2 - 4 People

Kimchijeon, also known as kimchi pancake, is a popular Korean dish celebrated for its bold flavors and satisfying texture. Made primarily with kimchi, a traditional Korean fermented vegetable (often napa cabbage) mixed with spicy seasonings, this dish embodies the essence of Korean culinary traditions. Its origins are deeply rooted in Korean households as an easy way to use up older or extra-fermented kimchi, turning it into a savory, crispy treat. To prepare kimchijeon, kimchi is mixed with a simple batter of flour and water, sometimes with the addition of starch for extra crispiness. The batter is often seasoned lightly since kimchi itself is packed with flavor. Many variations include additional ingredients such as sliced onions, scallions, or even seafood like squid or shrimp, enhancing its taste and texture. The mixture is then pan-fried to a golden-brown crispness, creating a dish that is both crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside.


Nutrition Info:

Calories: 200g, Fat: 7g, Carbs: 28g, Sodium: 520mg, Protein: 5g & Fiber: 2g


In Korean culture, kimchijeon is a comfort food often shared during gatherings or rainy days, as the act of frying pancakes resonates with the cozy ambiance of wet weather. With its delightful blend of simplicity and flavor, kimchijeon remains a timeless staple in Korean cuisine, celebrated by food enthusiasts worldwide.
